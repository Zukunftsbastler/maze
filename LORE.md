# Worldbuilding & Lore: The Antiquarian's Expeditions

## 1. The Premise
The city above is heavy. For centuries, the metropolis has been built upon its own ruins, slowly crushing the older districts into the mud. Beneath the bustling streets lies a forgotten, chaotic underworld of sunken guild halls, flooded crypts, and sealed arcane vaults. 

You play as **The Antiquarian**—part academic, part adventurer. Driven by an insatiable curiosity and a slightly reckless dedication to historical preservation, you delve into these unstable depths to rescue lost artifacts before they are crushed by the shifting earth forever. 

## 2. The Expedition System (Quests & Runs)
You don't just wander blindly. From the safety of your study, you select **Expedition Charters** (Quests). 
* **Risk and Reward:** Charters vary in length. A quick raid might only consist of 2 or 3 shallow depths, suitable for gathering basic resources. A deep dive might require surviving 7 or 8 increasingly brutal levels, promising legendary artifacts.
* **Preparation:** Each Charter provides a rough briefing of the targeted district and, crucially, hints about the specific entity guarding it.
* **The Descent:** The undercity is unstable. Corridors are blocked by centuries of debris. The maze generates dynamically because you are actively forcing doors and clearing rubble in real-time. 
* **The Breather:** Between each depth, you find a temporary "Safe Room" (an intact stairwell or a reinforced cellar). Here, you take a quick breath, choose your next route downward (multiple exits), and prepare for the Nemesis to mutate.
* **The Surface (Meta-Progression):** If you survive the final depth of your chosen Charter, you return to your **Curiosity Cabinet & Laboratory**. Here, your salvaged artifacts unlock permanent upgrades or new tools for future expeditions.

## 3. The Nemesis System
There is no generic enemy. Every Expedition is dominated by a **single, unique Nemesis** bound to that specific Charter. 

* **The Inevitable Hunt:** The Nemesis does not need a trail of chalk or a scent to track you. The undercity is its domain. It simply *knows* you are there, an inevitable force closing in on your position as you disturb the ancient dust.
* **Evolution:** As you push deeper into the run, the Nemesis adapts. Surviving early levels is easy, but with each new depth, the Nemesis mutates, growing faster, breaking your traps more easily, or gaining new abilities to counter your playstyle.

## 4. Example Expedition Charters

### Charter A: The Grand Archive Retrieval
* **Depth:** 4 Levels (Medium Risk)
* **The Environment:** Dry, dusty, filled with towering, collapsing bookshelves and shifting masonry.
* **The Nemesis: The Brass Custodian.** A hulking, clockwork security construct. It doesn't run, but it is an unstoppable juggernaut that easily smashes through basic barricades.
* **Briefing Note:** *"The Guild’s old Custodian still patrols the lower stacks. It's slow, but don't try to lock it out—it will tear the doors off their hinges. We need to be fast and use environmental hazards to stall it."*

### Charter B: The Sunken Vaults of House Blackwater
* **Depth:** 7 Levels (High Risk)
* **The Environment:** Flooded, slippery cobblestones with water hazards and rusted pipes.
* **The Nemesis: The Sludge Amalgam.** An amorphous mass of toxic runoff. It moves incredibly fast through water tiles and ignores physical barricades by oozing through the cracks.
* **Briefing Note:** *"The deeper we go, the more flooded it gets. The Amalgam thrives down there. Physical barriers won't hold it for long; we need to rely on alchemical fire and freezing traps to slow its advance."*

## 5. Mechanics in Lore
* **Buildspots & Defenses:** You trigger ancient infrastructure instead of building from scratch. Ignite unstable gas vents, knock over rotten support beams, or spill alchemy barrels to create hazards.
* **Artifacts (Upgrades):** Found during the run to help you survive. Examples: *Rogue's Boots* (faster traversal over rough terrain) or *Flashpowder* (temporarily stuns the Nemesis).
